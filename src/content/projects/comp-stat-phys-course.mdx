I designed and taught a week-long, Python-based course introducing **stochastic
simulation, entropy, phase transitions, and neural networks** to 20 high school
and undergraduate students from non-physics backgrounds at Tecnológico de
Monterrey.

The course was built around hands-on notebook labs that emphasized
model-building, data analysis, and physical intuition over heavy formalism.

## What we covered

- Random walks and Monte Carlo methods
- Probability, entropy, and the statistical view of disorder
- The Ising model and emergent collective behavior
- A gentle bridge from physical models to neural networks
