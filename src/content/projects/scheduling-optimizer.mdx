I built a public scheduling tool for Emory University's Residence Life staff,
reducing manual rota construction from hours to seconds.

## The problem

Staff scheduling looks simple until you write down the rules: people have
availability windows, every shift needs coverage, and the workload should be
*fair*. Done by hand, it took hours and still left someone unhappy.

## The approach

I modeled availability, coverage, and fairness as a **constrained combinatorial
optimization problem** and solved it with **warm-start stochastic local search** —
start from a reasonable guess, then make small improving swaps until the schedule
satisfies the constraints and balances the load.

## Outcome

- Cut schedule construction from hours to seconds.
- Shipped as a tool non-technical staff could actually use.
